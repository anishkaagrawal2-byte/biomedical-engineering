Introduction

Biomaterials are natural or synthetic materials used in biomedical engineering for medical devices, implants, prosthetics, and tissue engineering applications. They must be biocompatible and possess the required mechanical and chemical properties.

Main Types of Biomaterials

  • Metals
  • Polymers
  • Ceramics
  • Composites

Applications

  • Orthopedic implants (hip/knee replacements)
  • Dental implants
  • Cardiovascular stents and valves
  • Tissue scaffolds for regenerative medicine
  • Drug delivery systems

Recent Advances

Research focuses on smart biomaterials, bioactive coatings, biodegradable polymers, 3D-printed scaffolds, and nanomaterials for enhanced tissue regeneration and improved biocompatibility.
